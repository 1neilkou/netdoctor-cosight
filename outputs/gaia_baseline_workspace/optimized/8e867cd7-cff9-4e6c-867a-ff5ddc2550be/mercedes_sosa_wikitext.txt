'''Haydée Marta Mercedes Sosa Girón''' (9 July 1935<ref name="birth">[http://www.brainyhistory.com/events/1935/july_9_1935_93941.html Mercedes Sosa] at BrainyHistory.com</ref> – 4 October 2009) was an Argentine singer who was popular throughout [[Latin America]] and many countries outside the region. With her roots in [[Folk music of Argentina|Argentine folk music]], Sosa became one of the preeminent exponents of [[Nueva canción|El nuevo cancionero]]. She gave voice to songs written by many Latin American songwriters. She was hailed as the "voice of the voiceless ones", <ref name="tmc">{{cite web |title=Singer Mercedes Sosa: The voice of the 'voiceless ones' outlasts South American dictatorships |url=http://www.tmcnet.com/usubmit/2007/12/08/3150199.htm}}</ref> and often called "the conscience of Latin America".<ref name="Heckman">{{Cite web |last=Heckman |first=Don |date=29 October 1995 |title=POP MUSIC : The Voice Heard Round the World : Mercedes Sosa, a compelling figure in world music and a social activist, will make a rare L.A. appearance. |url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1995-10-29-ca-62462-story.html |access-date=5 December 2023 |website=Los Angeles Times |language=en-US}}</ref>

Sosa performed in venues such as the [[Lincoln Center]] in New York City, the [[Théâtre Mogador]] in Paris, the [[Sistine Chapel]] in Vatican City, as well as sold-out shows in New York's [[Carnegie Hall]] and the Roman [[Colosseum]] during her final decade of life. Her career spanned four decades and she was the recipient of six [[Latin Grammy]] awards (2000, 2003, 2004, 2006, 2009, 2011), including a [[Latin Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award]] in 2004 and two posthumous [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Folk Album]] in 2009 and 2011. She won the [[Premios Gardel|Premio Gardel]] in 2000, the main musical award in Argentina. She served as an ambassador for [[UNICEF]].

==Life==
Sosa was born on 9 July 1935, in [[San Miguel de Tucumán]], in the [[Argentine Northwest|northwestern Argentine]] province of [[Tucumán Province|Tucumán]], of [[mestizo]] ancestry. She was of French, Spanish and [[Diaguita]] descent.<ref name="Legendary folk singer Mercedes Sosa dies at 74"/> Her nickname "la negra", which is a common nickname in Argentina for people with darker complexion, is a reference to her indigenous heritage.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |last=Bernstein |first=Adam |date=5 October 2009 |title=Argentine folk singer who championed social justice |url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-2009-oct-05-me-mercedes-sosa5-story.html |access-date=8 March 2025 |website=Los Angeles Times |language=en-US}}</ref> Her parents, a day laborer and a washerwoman,<ref name="Heckman"/> were [[Peronism|Peronists]], although they never registered in the party, and she started her career as a singer for the [[Peronist Party]] in [[Tucumán Province|Tucuman]] under the name Gladys Osorio.<ref>''Mercedes Sosa: The Voice of Latin America''. Dir. Rodrigo H. Villa. First Run Features, 2013. Web.</ref> In 1950, at age fifteen, she won a singing competition organized by a local radio station and was given a contract to perform for two months.<ref name="Mercedes Sosa: Obituary"/> She recorded her first album, ''[[La voz de la zafra|La Voz de la Zafra]]'', in 1959.<ref name="Mercedes Sosa: Obituary"/> A performance at the 1965 [[Cosquín Festival|Cosquín National Folklore Festival]]—where she was introduced and brought to the stage while sitting in the audience by fellow folk singer [[Jorge Cafrune]]—<ref name="test">{{YouTube|QzwL8C2hE6c|The presentation by Jorge Cafrune and the song Mercedes Sosa sang}}. Retrieved 3 March 2010.</ref> brought her to the attention of the Argentine public.<ref name="Mercedes Sosa: Obituary" /> Sosa and her first husband, [[Manuel Oscar Matus]], with whom she had one son, were key players in the mid-60s ''[[nueva canción]]'' movement (which was called ''nuevo cancionero'' in Argentina).<ref name="Latin artist Mercedes Sosa dies" /> Her second record was ''Canciones con Fundamento'', a collection of Argentine folk songs.

[[File:Mercedes Sosa, Félix Luna y Ariel Ramírez.jpg|left|thumb|Sosa with [[Félix Luna]] and [[Ariel Ramírez]] (at the piano)]]
Sosa "spent the late 1960s building her audience in Europe and among the cosmopolitan middle class in Buenos Aires, becoming in the process a much bigger star" than her contemporaries.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Karush |first=Matthew |title=Musicians in Transit: Argentina and the Globalization of Popular Music |publisher=Duke |year=2017 |isbn=978-0-8223-7377-3 |pages=168}}</ref> In 1967, Sosa toured the United States and Europe with great success.{{Citation needed|date=December 2023}} In later years, she performed and recorded extensively, broadening her repertoire to include material from throughout Latin America.

In the early 1970s, Sosa released two concept albums in collaboration with composer [[Ariel Ramírez]] and lyricist [[Félix Luna]]: ''Cantata Sudamericana'' and ''Mujeres Argentinas'' (Argentine Women). She also recorded a tribute to Chilean musician [[Violeta Parra]] in 1971, including what was to become one of Sosa's signature songs, ''[[Gracias a la vida]]''.<ref name="Legendary folk singer Mercedes Sosa dies at 74"/><ref name=ap/> She further popularized of songs written by [[Milton Nascimento]] of Brazil and [[Pablo Milanés]] and [[Silvio Rodríguez]] both from [[Cuba]].<ref name="Legendary folk singer Mercedes Sosa dies at 74"/> Throughout the decade, she released albums such as ''Hasta la Victoria'' in 1972 and ''Traigo un Pueblo'' ''en mi Voz'' in 1973. They featured songs like "Cuando tenga la tierra", written by [[Ariel Petrocelli]] and Daniel Toro, which tackles political and social issues like wealth and land inequality.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web |title=Biografía |url=https://www.mercedessosa.org/biografia/ |access-date=8 March 2025 |website=Fundación Mercedes Sosa |language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web  |title=El folclore argentino llora la muerte de Daniel Toro - Notas - Viva la Radio |url=https://www.cadena3.com/noticia/viva-la-radio/murio-daniel-toro-icono-del-folclore-argentino_359018 |access-date=14 March 2025 |website=Cadena 3 Argentina |language=es}}</ref> During the 1970s she was a part of two films by the director [[Leopoldo Torre Nilsson]]: ''[[El Santo de la Espada]]'' in 1970 and ''[[Güemes: la tierra en armas|Güemes, la tierra en armas]]'' in 1971, in which she portrayed [[Juana Azurduy de Padilla]], the guerrilla military leader who fought for Argentine independence.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Citation |last=Nilsson |first=Leopoldo Torre |title=Güemes - la tierra en armas |date=7 April 1971 |type=Drama, History |url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0067165/?ref_=nm_knf_t_2 |access-date=8 March 2025 |others=Alfredo Alcón, Norma Aleandro, Gabriela Gili |publisher=Producciones Cinematográficas Cerrillos}}</ref>

[[File:Mercedes Sosa 2.jpg|thumb|Sosa in 1972]]
After the [[National Reorganization Process|military junta]] of [[Jorge Videla]] came to power in 1976, the atmosphere in Argentina grew increasingly oppressive. Sosa faced death threats against both her and her family, but refused for many years to leave the country. At a concert in [[La Plata]] in 1979, Sosa was searched and arrested on stage, along with all those attending the concert.<ref name="Latin artist Mercedes Sosa dies"/> Their release came about through international intervention.<ref name="Mercedes Sosa: Obituary"/> Despite attempts to hold more concerts, she was officially barred from performing by the military regime.<ref name=":0" /> Banned in her own country, she moved to Paris and then to [[Madrid]].<ref name="Mercedes Sosa: Obituary"/><ref name="Latin artist Mercedes Sosa dies"/> She has spoken publicly about her artistic and emotional struggles during this period of her life.<ref name=":0" /> While in exile, she released the album ''A Quien Doy'' in 1981. The album included a recording of the song "Cuando Me Acuerdo de Mi Pais" which was originally written by the prolific Chilean singer/songwriter, [[Patricio Manns]]. The song, which he wrote while also in political exile, expresses the sorrow he felt from being separated from his homeland.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Rodrigo |date=10 September 2020 |title=Patricio Manns: Cuando me acuerdo de mi país (1983) {{!}} PERRERAC: La canción, un arma de la revolución |url=https://perrerac.org/album/patricio-manns-cuando-me-acuerdo-de-mi-pais-1983/13772/ |access-date=14 March 2025 |language=es}}</ref> She related to this feeling and struggled to continue recording and performing. In an interview with the New York Times, she said, “It was a mental problem, a problem of morale...It wasn’t my throat, or anything physical".<ref name=":0" />

Sosa returned to Argentina from her exile in Europe in February 1982,<ref name=":1">{{Cite news |last=Lopez |first=Vicente F. |date=18 January 1983 |title=ARTISTAS EXILIADOS HAN REGRESADO A ARGENTINA |url=https://infoweb.newsbank.com/apps/news/openurl?ctx_ver=z39.88-2004&rft_id=info%3Asid/infoweb.newsbank.com&svc_dat=WORLDNEWS&req_dat=0D1C15DBC6335C60&rft_val_format=info%3Aofi/fmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Actx&rft_dat=document_id%3Anews%252F0EB960FAFAAF7A0F |access-date=7 March 2025 |work=El Nuevo Herald |pages=8}}</ref><ref name="Latin artist Mercedes Sosa dies"/> several months before the military regime collapsed as a result of the [[Falklands War]], and gave a series of concerts at the ''[[Teatro Opera|Teatro Ópera]]'' in Buenos Aires, where she invited many of her younger colleagues to share the stage. A [[double album]] of recordings, ''[[Mercedes Sosa en Argentina]],'' from these performances became an instant best seller. She then traveled to perform in her home province of Tucuman. However, these performances were largely ignored by mainstream media in the country.<ref name=":1" /> In subsequent years, Sosa continued to tour both in Argentina and abroad, performing in such venues as the [[Lincoln Center]] in New York City and the ''[[Théâtre Mogador]]'' in Paris. In poor health for much of the 1990s, she performed a comeback show in Argentina in 1998.<ref name="Mercedes Sosa: Obituary"/> In 1994, she played in the [[Sistine Chapel]] in Vatican City.<ref name="Legendary folk singer Mercedes Sosa dies at 74">{{cite web |url=http://www.france24.com/en/20091004-legendary-folk-singer-mercedes-sosa-dies-74-argentina-entertainment-music |title=Legendary folk singer Mercedes Sosa dies at 74 |date=4 October 2009 |access-date=5 October 2009 |publisher=[[France 24]]}}</ref> In 2002, she sold out both [[Carnegie Hall]] in New York and the [[Colosseum]] in Rome in the same year.<ref name="Legendary folk singer Mercedes Sosa dies at 74"/>

[[File:Mercedes Sosa.jpg|thumb|left|Sosa in 1973]]
A supporter of [[Juan Perón|Perón]], she favored leftist causes throughout her life. She supported President [[Raúl Alfonsín|Raul Alfonsin]] in the election of 1983 which marked the return of democracy in Argentina following the dictatorship. She referred to this election as "Argentina's Spring"<ref>{{Cite news |last=Drosdoff |first=Daniel |date=30 October 1983 |title=ARGENTINIAN VOTE TO END DICTATORSHIP PERONIST AND RADICAL IN LEAD FOR PRESIDENCY |url=https://infoweb.newsbank.com/apps/news/openurl?ctx_ver=z39.88-2004&rft_id=info%3Asid/infoweb.newsbank.com&svc_dat=WORLDNEWS&req_dat=0D1C15DBC6335C60&rft_val_format=info%3Aofi/fmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Actx&rft_dat=document_id%3Anews%252F0EB35DEF2EAA1BC5 |access-date=7 March 2025 |work=Miami Herald |pages=16A}}</ref> She opposed President [[Carlos Menem]], who was in office from 1989 to 1999, and supported the election of [[Néstor Kirchner]], who became president in 2003.<ref>[http://www.berlinonline.de/berliner-zeitung/archiv/.bin/dump.fcgi/2003/1025/magazin/0001/ Interview with Mercedes Sosa] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091016201953/http://www.berlinonline.de/berliner-zeitung/archiv/.bin/dump.fcgi/2003/1025/magazin/0001/ |date=16 October 2009 }}, ''Magazin Berliner Zeitung'', 25 October 2003. {{in lang|de}}</ref>
Sosa was a [[UNESCO Goodwill Ambassador]] for Latin America and the Caribbean.<ref name="Latin artist Mercedes Sosa dies"/><ref>[http://chicago.cervantes.es/Cultura/Fichas/Ficha41728_47_2.htm Mercedes Sosa in concert] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080104200631/http://chicago.cervantes.es/Cultura/Fichas/Ficha41728_47_2.htm |date=4 January 2008 }}</ref>

Sosa disliked being identified as a protest singer.<ref name="Heckman"/><ref>{{Cite web |last=Meyer |first=Bill |date=7 October 2009 |title=A U.S. musician pays tribute to Mercedes Sosa |url=https://www.peoplesworld.org/article/a-u-s-musician-pays-tribute-to-mercedes-sosa/ |access-date=5 December 2023 |website=People's World |language=en-US}}</ref> While she was outright in her political stances, Sosa said the following on the position of the artist:

{{Blockquote|text=“An artist isn’t political in the party political sense – they have a constituency, which is their public – it is the poetry that matters most of all.”|author=|source=}}

In a career spanning four decades, she worked with performers across several genres and generations, folk, opera, pop, rock, including [[Martha Argerich]], [[Andrea Bocelli]], [[David Broza]], [[Franco Battiato]], [[Jaime Roos]], [[Joan Baez]], [[Francis Cabrel]], [[Gal Costa]], [[Luz Casal]], [[Lila Downs]], [[Lucio Dalla]], [[Maria Farantouri]], [[Lucecita Benitez]], [[Nilda Fernández]], [[Charly Garcia]], [[León Gieco]], [[Gian Marco]], [[Nana Mouskouri]], [[Pablo Milanés]], [[Holly Near]], [[Milton Nascimento]], [[Pata Negra]], [[Fito Páez]], [[Franco De Vita]], [[Lourdes Pérez]], [[Luciano Pavarotti]], [[Silvio Rodríguez]], [[Ismael Serrano]], [[Shakira]], [[Sting (musician)|Sting]], [[Caetano Veloso]],<ref name="Legendary folk singer Mercedes Sosa dies at 74"/> [[Julieta Venegas]], [[Gustavo Cerati]] and [[Konstantin Wecker]]<ref name="Latin artist Mercedes Sosa dies">{{cite web |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/8289370.stm |title=Latin artist Mercedes Sosa dies |date=4 October 2009 |access-date=5 October 2009 |publisher=[[BBC]]}}</ref>

Sosa participated in a 1999 production of [[Ariel Ramírez]]'s ''Misa Criolla''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://soundsandcolours.com/articles/argentina/in-profile-mercedes-sosa-1761/ |title=In Profile: Mercedes Sosa |website=soundsandcolours.com |date=26 August 2010 |access-date=27 March 2018}}</ref> Her song ''Balderrama'' is featured in the 2008 movie ''[[Che (2008 film)|Che]]'', starring [[Benicio del Toro]] as the Argentine [[Marxist]] revolutionary [[Che Guevara]].<ref>{{YouTube|k4LJDTlviKw|''Balderrama'' by Mercedes Sosa}} – a tribute to [[Che Guevara]]</ref>

Sosa was the co-chair of the [[Earth Charter]] International Commission.

==Awards==
Sosa won the [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Folk Album]] in 2000 (''Misa Criolla''),<ref>{{Cite web |title=Latin Grammys: Ganadores – Años Anteriores (2000) |url=https://www.latingrammy.com/es/nominees/search?artist=&field_nominee_work_value=&year=2000&genre=All |access-date=7 July 2021 |website=Latin Grammys |publisher=The Latin Recording Academy |language=es}}</ref> 2003 (''Acústico''),<ref>{{Cite web |title=Latin Grammys: Ganadores – Años Anteriores (2003) |url=https://www.latingrammy.com/es/nominees/search?artist=&field_nominee_work_value=&year=2003&genre=All |access-date=7 July 2021 |website=Latin Grammys |publisher=The Latin Recording Academy |language=es}}</ref> 2006 (''Corazón Libre''),<ref>{{Cite web |title=Latin Grammys: Ganadores – Años Anteriores (2006) |url=https://www.latingrammy.com/es/nominees/search?artist=&field_nominee_work_value=&year=2006&genre=All |access-date=7 July 2021 |website=Latin Grammys |publisher=The Latin Recording Academy |language=es}}</ref> 2009 (''[[Cantora, un Viaje Íntimo|Cantora 1]]'', which also won [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Recording Package|Best Recording Package]] and was nominated for [[Latin Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]]),<ref>{{Cite web |title=Latin Grammys: Ganadores – Años Anteriores (2009) |url=https://www.latingrammy.com/es/nominees/search?artist=&field_nominee_work_value=&year=2009&genre=All |access-date=7 July 2021 |website=Latin Grammys |publisher=The Latin Recording Academy |language=es}}</ref> and 2011 (''Deja La Vida Volar''),<ref>{{Cite web |title=Latin Grammys: Ganadores – Años Anteriores (2011) |url=https://www.latingrammy.com/es/nominees/search?artist=&field_nominee_work_value=&year=2011&genre=All |access-date=7 July 2021 |website=Latin Grammys |publisher=The Latin Recording Academy |language=es}}</ref> as well as several international awards.

In 1995, [[Konex Foundation]] from Argentina granted her the Diamond [[Konex Award]], one of the most prestigious awards in Argentina, as the most important personality in the popular music of her country in the last decade.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Premios Konex 1995: Música Popular |url=https://www.fundacionkonex.org/premios1995-musica-popular |access-date=7 July 2021 |website=Fundación Konex |language=es}}</ref>

==Death==
[[File:Funeral de Mercedes Sosa.jpg|thumb|Mercedes Sosa lying in repose, with her family and President [[Cristina Fernández de Kirchner]] viewing]]
Suffering from recurrent [[Endocrine system|endocrine]] and [[Respiratory system|respiratory]] problems in later years, the 74-year-old Sosa was hospitalized in Buenos Aires on 18 September 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clarin.com/diario/2009/10/01/um/m-02010016.htm|title="En ningún momento sufrió", dijo el hijo de Mercedes Sosa|date=October 2009|language=es|access-date=1 October 2009|archive-date=4 October 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091004144610/http://www.clarin.com/diario/2009/10/01/um/m-02010016.htm|url-status=dead}}</ref> She died from [[multiple organ failure]] on 4 October 2009, at 5:15 am.<ref name=ap>[https://www.google.com/hostednews/ap/article/ALeqM5iBSb5ZdEJ8MI6_qzWccZNZQITnMAD9B47Q8G1 Associated Press]{{dead link|date=June 2024|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref> She was survived by one son, Fabián Matus (d. 15 March 2019), born of her first marriage.<ref name="Mercedes Sosa: Obituary"/><ref name="Argentine singer Mercedes Sosa, 'voice of Latin America,' dies at 74"/> He said: "She lived her 74 years to the fullest. She had done practically everything she wanted, she didn't have any type of barrier or any type of fear that limited her".<ref name="Argentine singer Mercedes Sosa, 'voice of Latin America,' dies at 74">{{cite web|author=Javier Doberti|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2009/SHOWBIZ/Music/10/04/obit.mercedes.sosa/|title=Argentine singer Mercedes Sosa, 'voice of Latin America,' dies at 74|date=4 October 2009|access-date=5 October 2009|publisher=[[CNN]]}}</ref> The hospital expressed its sympathies to her relatives.<ref name="Argentine folk legend Mercedes Sosa dead at 74">{{cite news|url=http://www.bangkokpost.com/news/world/156066/argentine-folk-legend-mercedes-sosa-dead-at-74|title=Argentine folk legend Mercedes Sosa dead at 74|date=4 October 2009|access-date=5 October 2009|newspaper=[[Bangkok Post]]}}</ref> Her website featured the following: "Her undisputed talent, her honesty and her profound convictions leave a great legacy to future generations".<ref name="Argentine folk icon Sosa dies at 74"/>

Her body was placed on display at the [[Argentine National Congress|National Congress]] building in Buenos Aires for the public to pay their respects, and President Fernández de Kirchner ordered three days of national mourning.<ref name="Argentine singer Mercedes Sosa, 'voice of Latin America,' dies at 74"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lanacion.com.ar/nota.asp?nota_id=1182377&pid=7460859&toi=6255|title=Continúa la procesión en el Congreso para despedir a Mercedes Sosa}}</ref> Thousands had queued by the end of the day.<ref name="Argentine folk icon Sosa dies at 74">{{cite news|url=http://english.aljazeera.net/news/americas/2009/10/200910421057755554.html#|title=Argentine folk icon Sosa dies at 74|date=4 October 2009|access-date=5 October 2009|publisher=[[Al Jazeera Arabic|Al Jazeera]]}}</ref><ref name="Argentine singer Mercedes Sosa dies at 74">{{cite news|author=Helen Popper|url=http://in.reuters.com/article/hollywood/idINTRE5931S220091004|title=Argentine singer Mercedes Sosa dies at 74|date=4 October 2009|access-date=5 October 2009|work=[[Reuters]]|archive-date=11 October 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091011000305/http://in.reuters.com/article/hollywood/idINTRE5931S220091004|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Sosa's obituary in ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]'' said she was "an unrivalled interpreter of works by her compatriot, the Argentine [[Atahualpa Yupanqui]], and Chile's [[Violeta Parra]]".<ref name="Mercedes Sosa: Obituary">{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/culture-obituaries/music-obituaries/6259898/Mercedes-Sosa.html|title=Mercedes Sosa: Obituary|date=4 October 2009|access-date=5 October 2009|newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph]]}}</ref> Helen Popper of [[Reuters]] reported her death by saying she "fought South America's dictators with her voice and became a giant of contemporary Latin American music".<ref name="Argentine singer Mercedes Sosa dies at 74"/> Sosa received three [[Latin Grammy]] nominations for her album, in 2009 . She went on to win Best Folk Album about a month after her death.<ref name="Legendary folk singer Mercedes Sosa dies at 74"/><ref name="Latin artist Mercedes Sosa dies"/>
