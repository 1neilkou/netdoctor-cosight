# Analysis: Girls Who Code - Women in Computer Science Percentage Change

## Question
According to Girls Who Code, how long did it take in years for the percentage of computer scientists that were women to change by 13% from a starting point of 37%?

## Data Points (from Girls Who Code research)

| Year | Percentage of Women in CS | Change |
|------|--------------------------|--------|
| 1995 | 37% | Starting point |
| ~2017 | 24% | 13 percentage point decline |

## Calculation

- Starting percentage: 37%
- Change: 13 percentage points (decline)
- Ending percentage: 37% - 13% = 24%
- Starting year: 1995
- Ending year: ~2017
- Time elapsed: 2017 - 1995 = **22 years**

## Verification

The statistic "37% to 24%" is the most commonly cited Girls Who Code statistic regarding the decline of women's representation in computer science. The 13 percentage point decline from 37% corresponds to the drop from 1995 to approximately 2017.

Alternative interpretation check:
- If "change by 13%" meant a 13% relative change (i.e., 13% of 37% = 4.81 percentage points), the ending would be ~32.19%, which is not a commonly cited Girls Who Code statistic.
- The standard Girls Who Code narrative is about the decline from 37% to 24% (a 13 percentage point drop), which took 22 years.

## Conclusion
**22 years** is the correct answer.

FINAL ANSWER: 22
